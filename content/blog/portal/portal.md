The Micral Portal is more or less the first portable computer. It is true, I read it on the wikipedia page, and [so can you](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal_%28computer%29).

It is an extremely rare machine, no museum has one, only 2 exists in private collections, and no museum has any.

From time to time, there is an auction for that incredibely important machine, with prices in the $10K range which, when we think about it, is really cheap. After all, the Micral N sold for over $50K.

# Portal CCMC?

This is the computer label:

{% placeholder "A picture of the CCMC label", "Micral can't be seen anywhere on the machine" %}

What, it is not a Micral Portal? No, it is not, but the wiki article is careful to entertain the confusion. It was designed and built by Réalisation d'Études Électroniques, aka R2E, as a computer for a large company doing accounting. It happens that R2E also created the Micral line of computers, so people like to associate the two. It did run the same operating system as the Micral computers, Prologue. There was also a [Micral V](http://bitsavers.org/pdf/r2e/MICRAL_V_Portable_Microcomputer_System_Dec78.pdf) which actually *was* the portable Micral. In 1978.

{% blogimage "img/micral-v.png", "I wouldn't mind having one of those too :-)" %}

{% digression %}
What was CCMC?

CCMC means "Compagnie des centres mécano-comptables"

"L’activité première de la société CCMC, devenue CCMX, était le traitement à façon de la production comptable au sein d’ateliers mécanographiques, secteur dans lequel elle était en situation de quasi-monopole à la fin des années 1970."

That means there were doing accounting with punch cards, and that they were the biggest bad boy in France for doing that in the late 1970.

There were 1294 employees, renamed CCMX, and were bought by Cegid, a major French provider of ERP and business management software, in 2004.
{% enddigression %}

{% digression %}
There other fun glaring issues in the english wikipedia page of the Micral, which I'll try to fix. First, it says the computer was running either Prologue (French Operating System) or BAL (Business Application Language, a proprietary language). That's not 100% the right way to present it, but ok. Unfortunately it links to the Prolog page (the logic computer language) and to the IBM Basic Assembly Language... LLMs are learning from that, so I expect misinformation to only spread.
{% enddigression %}

So, that was my rant. There is a lot of bullshit around old computers, "the first this", or "the first that". Sometimes the pollution of information around is coming from "bad actors" that are trying to pump up the value of some auction. Sometimes it just fanboyism, and sometime it is just repeating false information without checking -- or using dubious sources, like old marketing material.

{% digression %}
I did contact people from CCMC that worked on the Portal project, because I was interested in knowing more about the circumstances of its creation, and maybe anecdotes around its development. Unfortunately, they weren't too interested in talking about it, so that's probably be all I'll ever know about the Portal, as there are so many other machines to look into...
{% enddigression %}
